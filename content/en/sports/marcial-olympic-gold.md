PARIS — Eumir Felix Marcial delivered a performance of controlled fury Saturday night, stunning the 15,000-strong crowd at the Roland Garros Arena to win a split decision over Cuba's Lazaro Alvarez and claim the Philippines' first Olympic boxing gold medal in 30 years.

The scores read 5-4, 4-5, 5-4 — a cliff-hanger that had fans in Zamboanga City and across the Philippines holding their breath until the final scorecard was announced.

## The Fight

Marcial, 28, deployed the tactics his trainer Roel Velasco had drilled for three years: a tight guard to neutralize Alvarez's crisp Cuban jab, relentless body work in the clinch, and sudden explosive uppercut combinations in the final 30 seconds of each round to impress the judges.

Alvarez had his moments — particularly in the fourth and seventh rounds where his footwork created openings for left hooks to the temple — but Marcial's superior conditioning told in the championship rounds, as the Cuban began to take extra time getting up after clinches.

## Reaction

"I told myself: every Filipino fighter who never got their chance gave me their strength tonight," Marcial said through tears at the post-fight press conference, the gold medal glinting under the arena lights. "This belongs to all of them."

President Marcos sent a congratulatory message from Manila and announced that Marcial would receive the Presidential Medal of Merit, a condominium unit, and the ₱10 million government bonus mandated for Olympic gold medalists under Republic Act 10699.
