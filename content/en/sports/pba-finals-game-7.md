Tip-off is at 7 p.m. and 18,000 seats at the SM Mall of Asia Arena are already sold out. Tonight's Game 7 of the 2026 PBA Philippine Cup Finals between the San Miguel Beermen and the Magnolia Chicken Timplados is, by any measure, the most anticipated basketball game in Philippine professional sports in at least a decade.

The series has been a masterclass in contrasting styles: San Miguel's grinding, half-court physicality versus Magnolia's uptempo pace-and-space attack. Each team has won games by comfortable margins, and each has stolen a win on the opponent's floor. Everything comes down to 48 minutes tonight.

## Series Recap

| Game | Winner | Score |
|------|--------|-------|
| Game 1 | San Miguel | 98–87 |
| Game 2 | Magnolia | 91–89 |
| Game 3 | Magnolia | 103–97 OT |
| Game 4 | San Miguel | 88–82 |
| Game 5 | San Miguel | 95–90 |
| Game 6 | Magnolia | 101–94 |

The series MVP race has been a two-man battle between San Miguel's CJ Perez — who is averaging 26.4 points, 6.2 rebounds and 4.8 assists — and Magnolia's Ian Sangalang, who has been a force in the paint with 22.1 points and 11.7 rebounds per game.

## Keys to the Game

**For San Miguel:** Head coach Leo Austria wants his team to attack Magnolia's zone with skip passes and early offense before the defense can set. Stopping Sangalang from getting deep post position will be equally critical.

**For Magnolia:** Head coach Chito Victolero has emphasized ball movement and limiting turnovers — Magnolia has lost every game in this series where it committed 15 or more turnovers. Point guard Paul Lee needs to control tempo and avoid foul trouble.

## Star Power in the Crowd

Philippine basketball royalty will be in attendance: former NBA player Andray Blatche is expected courtside, and several UAAP legends have confirmed they will be watching. The MVP and SMC corporate brass will occupy opposite ends of the arena in what promises to be the most electric atmosphere in recent PBA memory.
