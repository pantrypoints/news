Dave Ildefonso was almost unstoppable Wednesday evening at the Big Dome, combining an efficient shooting performance with six assists and five steals to lead the Ateneo Blue Eagles to a 78–71 victory over the FEU Tamaraws, keeping their unblemished record intact at 10-0 in UAAP Season 89.

But if Ateneo coach Tab Baldwin was completely satisfied, his post-game press conference demeanor didn't show it.

"FEU gave us everything we could handle in the second half," Baldwin said. "Their energy, their physicality — it exposed things we need to clean up before the Final Four."

## FEU's Second-Half Stand

Trailing by 17 at halftime, FEU coach Olsen Racela made adjustments that turned the game into a showcase. Guard RJ Abarrientos — the transfer from Kentucky whom observers had dubbed an "American import" in college shorts — erupted for 18 points in the third quarter alone, slashing through Ateneo's defense and drawing the gap to 5 with 4 minutes remaining.

Ildefonso then responded with the play of the game: a step-back three over two defenders with the shot clock expiring that restored the Eagles' cushion and effectively broke FEU's spirit.
