The Social Security System (SSS) disclosed Monday that a ransomware group identifying itself as "Phantom Circuit" accessed its member database servers on March 6, encrypting backup records and exfiltrating what it claims is a 2.3-terabyte archive of personal member data covering 9.4 million individuals.

The breach is the largest government data incident in Philippine history by affected member count, eclipsing the 2016 COMELEC leak that exposed voter records of approximately 55 million Filipinos.

## What Was Compromised

SSS President and Chief Executive Officer Rolando Macasaet confirmed the following data fields were included in the breach:

- Full name, date of birth, and home address
- SSS number and employer information
- Monthly contribution history
- Active loan balances and payment history
- Bank account details for members enrolled in direct credit programs (approximately 1.2 million members)

The agency stressed that its pension disbursement system — a separate, air-gapped infrastructure — was **not** affected, and that pensions and benefit releases would proceed normally.

## The Ransom Demand

Phantom Circuit published a 15-minute sample data dump on a dark web forum and demanded $4 million in the privacy-focused cryptocurrency Monero within 72 hours, threatening to auction the full database to the highest bidder on underground marketplaces.

Macasaet said the SSS would not pay the ransom. The Cybercrime Investigation and Coordinating Center (CICC) and the National Bureau of Investigation (NBI) Cybercrime Division have opened parallel investigations.

## Protect Yourself

SSS members should immediately:

1. Monitor bank accounts linked to their SSS records for unauthorized transactions
2. Place a fraud alert with their bank's anti-fraud department
3. Enable two-factor authentication on their SSS online account at my.sss.gov.ph
4. Be wary of phishing calls or SMS messages claiming to be from SSS

The National Privacy Commission (NPC) has 72 hours to conduct a formal breach notification assessment and will issue specific guidance for affected members.
